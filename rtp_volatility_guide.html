The Mathematics Behind Casino Games

What is RTP?

RTP stands for "Return to Player" and represents the percentage of all wagered money that a casino game will pay back to players over time. It's one of the most important metrics for understanding any casino game.

TYPICAL SLOT RTP

96%

For every $100 wagered, the game returns $96 to players on average

How RTP Works in Practice

üìà Real-World Example

Imagine a slot machine with 96% RTP receives $1,000,000 in total bets from all players over a month:

Total Wagered: $1,000,000
RTP Rate: 96% (0.96)
Total Returned to Players: $1,000,000 √ó 0.96 = $960,000
Casino Keeps (House Edge): $1,000,000 - $960,000 = $40,000 (4%)

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: RTP is Calculated Over Millions of Spins

RTP is a long-term statistical average. In a single gaming session, your actual return can vary dramatically. You might win 500% or lose 100% of your bankroll. RTP only becomes accurate over millions of game rounds.

The House Edge Formula

The relationship between RTP and House Edge:

House Edge = 100% - RTP

Example: If RTP is 96%, then House Edge is 4%

Common Myths Debunked

‚ùå Myth: "A game is due to pay out"

Reality: Every spin is independent. Past results don't influence future outcomes. A game with 96% RTP doesn't have to "catch up" after paying out big‚Äîit maintains 96% over millions of spins naturally.

‚ùå Myth: "Higher volatility means lower RTP"

Reality: Volatility and RTP are independent. You can have high RTP with high volatility, or low RTP with low volatility. Always check both metrics.

‚ùå Myth: "You can beat RTP with strategy"

Reality: In purely chance-based games like slots and roulette, no strategy can overcome the house edge. RTP is mathematically fixed. (Note: Strategy does matter in skill games like blackjack and poker.)
