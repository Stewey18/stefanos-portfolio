What is Baccarat?

Baccarat (pronounced bah-kah-rah) is a comparing card game where you bet on which of two hands‚Äîthe "Player" or the "Banker"‚Äîwill have a total closest to 9. Despite its reputation as a high-stakes VIP game, baccarat is remarkably simple to learn and offers some of the best odds in the casino.

Important: You don't play against the dealer or other players. You simply bet on which hand you think will win. The dealer handles all the cards according to fixed rules.

How to Play: Complete Walkthrough

Game Flow

Step 1: Place Your Bets

Before cards are dealt, choose to bet on Player, Banker, or Tie. You can bet on multiple options simultaneously.

Step 2: Initial Deal

The dealer deals two cards to the Player hand and two cards to the Banker hand, all face-up.

Step 3: Check for Naturals

If either hand totals 8 or 9, it's called a "natural." The hand with the higher natural wins immediately. If both have naturals, the higher one wins (or it's a tie).

Step 4: Third Card Rules (If Needed)

If neither hand has a natural, the dealer follows strict rules to determine if a third card is drawn. You don't make any decisions‚Äîit's all automatic.

Step 5: Determine Winner

After all cards are dealt, the hand closest to 9 wins. Winning bets are paid out immediately.

Betting Strategy

üéØ Expert Recommendations

  • Always Bet on Banker: With a 1.06% house edge, the Banker bet offers the best odds in baccarat. The 5% commission on wins is worth it.
  • Avoid the Tie Bet: Despite its attractive 8:1 payout, the 14.36% house edge makes this a sucker bet. Only bet on ties for fun, not strategy.
  • Player Bet is Second Best: At 1.24% house edge, it's nearly as good as Banker. Some players alternate to avoid commission.
  • Ignore Pattern Tracking: Many players keep scorecards to spot "patterns," but each hand is independent. Past results don't predict future outcomes.
  • Set Win/Loss Limits: Decide before playing when you'll walk away after winning or losing a certain amount.
  • Flat Betting is Safest: Bet the same amount every hand rather than progressive systems like Martingale.
  • Commission Awareness: Remember that Banker wins pay 0.95:1 due to the 5% commission. Calculate your actual winnings accordingly.
