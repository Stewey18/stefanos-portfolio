Why Position is Your Biggest Edge

Position is the most important concept in poker. More important than hand strength, pot odds, or even reading opponents. Acting last gives you complete information about every player's action before making your decision‚Äîan insurmountable advantage.

The Position Profit Reality:
  • Button (BTN): +35 bb/100 average winrate for winning players
  • Cutoff (CO): +20 bb/100
  • Middle Position: +5 bb/100
  • Under the Gun: -2 to +3 bb/100
  • Small Blind: -15 to -20 bb/100 (even for pros!)

Middle Position (MP) - Moderate Expansion

Opening range: ~20% of hands

Add to UTG range:

  • 66, 55 (small pairs for set value)
  • AJ offsuit, KQ offsuit
  • QJs, JTs (suited Broadway connectors)
  • A9s-A7s (suited aces with wheel potential)

Hijack (HJ) - Good Stealing Position

Opening range: ~25% of hands

Add to MP range:

  • 44, 33, 22 (all pocket pairs)
  • AT offsuit, KJ offsuit
  • T9s, 87s, 76s (more suited connectors)
  • QTs, J9s (suited two-gap hands)

Cutoff (CO) - Premium Stealing Position

Opening range: ~35% of hands

Add to HJ range:

  • A9o, A8o, A7o (weaker aces)
  • KT offsuit, QJ offsuit
  • K9s, Q9s, J8s (suited connectors with gaps)
  • 65s, 54s (small suited connectors)
  • Suited kings: K8s, K7s, K6s

Button (BTN) - Widest Profitable Range

Opening range: ~45-50% of hands

Add to CO range:

  • A6o, A5o, A4o, A3o, A2o (all aces)
  • K9o, Q9o, JT offsuit
  • All suited Kings, Queens down to Q2s
  • 43s, 32s (all suited connectors)
  • J7s, T6s, 96s (suited one-gap hands)
Pro Tip: On the button vs weak opponents, you can profitably open almost 60% of hands. Against tight players who rarely defend blinds, open even wider. Position + fold equity = massive profit.

Small Blind Strategy

The small blind is the worst position in poker. You must play tighter than big blind because:

  • Only putting in 0.5bb (worse pot odds)
  • Big blind still to act behind you
  • Out of position postflop if BB calls
Small Blind Reality:
Even world-class players lose money from the small blind long-term. The goal is to minimize losses, not make profit. Fold approximately 70% of hands facing a button open.

Postflop Position Advantages

Information Advantage

Acting last provides massive advantages:

  • Pot Control: Check back when weak, build pot when strong
  • Bluff Efficiency: Only need to bluff when opponent shows weakness
  • Value Extraction: Can raise for value after opponent bets
  • Free Cards: Check behind to see turn/river without risk
  • Float Opportunities: Call with weak hands to steal later streets

The Sandwich Effect

Being "sandwiched" between raiser and caller is extremely disadvantageous:

Sandwich Situation Example:
UTG opens, MP calls, you're in CO with KJo.

Why this is bad:
  • UTG has strong range
  • MP shows interest (likely has something)
  • Button and blinds still to act behind you
  • You'll be out of position to MP if you call
  • Your hand strength is capped (no big pairs or AK)
Correct play: FOLD (even though KJo is playable in CO normally)

Exploiting Positional Weaknesses

Targeting Players Out of Position

When opponents limp from early position, they're signaling weakness and poor position awareness. Exploit relentlessly:

  • Iso-raise wide: Raise 3-5x with 30%+ of hands to punish limps
  • C-bet frequently: They'll check to raiser 70%+ of time
  • Triple barrel light: They can't call three streets with weak pairs

Position in Different Game Types

Cash Games

Position value is maximized in cash games due to deep stacks and ability to quit anytime. Always seek seats with aggressive players on your right and passive players on your left.

Tournaments

Position becomes even more valuable as stacks get shorter. With 20-30bb effective, button raises generate massive fold equity. At final tables, position determines who survives.

Fast-Fold Games

Position still matters but table dynamics don't. Can't exploit specific opponent tendencies, so stick to fundamental positional ranges.

Common Positional Mistakes

  1. Calling 3-bets out of position: Rarely profitable with non-premium hands
  2. Opening too wide from early position: Gets you in trouble postflop
  3. Not stealing enough from late position: Leaving massive profit on table
  4. Defending blinds too wide: Position disadvantage magnifies mistakes
  5. Cold-calling in middle seats: Gets sandwiched by button/blinds
  6. Not 3-betting enough IP: Missing chances to deny position
  7. Playing same range regardless of position: Fundamental error

Advanced Position Concepts

The Position Paradox

Early position opens signal strength precisely because of positional disadvantage. Therefore, opponents give you more respect UTG than on the button, even though your range should be tighter.

Relative Position

Absolute position matters less than relative position to the aggressor. If UTG opens and you're on the button with 87s:

  • You have absolute position (button)
  • BUT UTG has betting initiative
  • AND you're out of position to other callers postflop
  • Result: Speculative hands lose value

Conclusion

Position is the most valuable asset in poker. Good players win from late position and minimize losses from early position. Great players maximize the positional advantage until it becomes their primary profit source.

Master positional play and you'll beat opponents with better hand-reading skills, superior math knowledge, or tighter ranges. Because when you act with complete information and they don't, you're playing an entirely different‚Äîand vastly superior‚Äîgame.
