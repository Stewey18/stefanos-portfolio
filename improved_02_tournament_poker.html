Understanding Tournament Dynamics vs Cash Games

Tournament poker requires fundamentally different strategies than cash games. Your chips have fluctuating value based on your stack size, stage, and position relative to payouts.

Aspect Cash Games Tournaments
Chip Value Constant ($1 = $1) Fluctuating (ICM dependent)
Risk Taking Consistent Stage dependent
Stack Management Rebuy anytime Protect your stack
Time Pressure None Increasing blinds
Goal Maximize hourly rate Ladder up payouts

Early Stage Strategy (Levels 1-6, 100+ BB)

Optimal Early Stage Approach

The early stage is your opportunity to accumulate chips at lower risk. Deep stacks allow for more post-flop play and value extraction.

Early Stage Priorities:
  • Play tight-aggressive, standard ranges
  • Build chips through value betting, not bluffing
  • Target weak players for value
  • Avoid large pots without premium hands
  • Preserve stack for later stages

  • ‚ùå Getting married to one pair hands in large pots
  • ‚ùå Risking your stack without premium holdings

Middle Stage Strategy (Levels 7-12, 30-60 BB)

Adjusting to Medium Stacks

Middle stages require increased aggression and selective risk-taking. Antes are now in play, making pots worth stealing. Stack sizes determine your strategy.

Stack-Size Strategies:

Stack Size Strategy Key Adjustments
Short (10-20 BB) Push/fold mode Open-shove or fold preflop, limited post-flop
Medium (20-40 BB) Aggressive stealing Steal blinds/antes, 3-bet light, avoid large pots
Big (40-60 BB) Apply pressure Attack medium/short stacks, accumulate chips
Huge (60+ BB) Chip leader mode Maximum aggression, put others at risk

Push/Fold Charts (Short Stack Play)

When you're below 15 BB, simple push/fold strategy becomes optimal. Here are mathematically correct shoving ranges:

12 BB Shoving Ranges:
  • Button vs Blinds: Top 45% of hands (22+, A2+, K2s+, K7o+, Q5s+, Q9o+, J7s+, J9o+, T8s+, 98s, 87s)
  • Cutoff vs 3 players: Top 25% (44+, A7s+, A9o+, KTs+, KJo+, QJs)
  • Middle position: Top 15% (88+, ATs+, AJo+, KQs)
  • Early position: Top 8% (TT+, AJs+, AQo+)

Blind vs Blind Dynamics

Blind-on-blind battles are critical for chip accumulation in middle stages. Play aggressively from small blind:

  • SB vs BB with 30BB: Raise 40-50% of hands
  • SB vs BB with 20BB: Raise or shove 50-60% of hands
  • SB vs BB with 10BB: Shove 60-70% of hands
  • BB Defense vs SB: Defend 50-60% against standard raises

Bubble Play Strategy (Near Money Positions)

Understanding ICM Pressure

The bubble is where ICM (Independent Chip Model) has maximum impact. Chips lost hurt more than chips won help you.

ICM Example: In a tournament paying top 10%, being on the bubble with a medium stack, you should fold QQ to an all-in because busting costs you ~8% equity in the prize pool, but calling and winning only gains you ~3% equity. The risk-reward is skewed.

Bubble Strategy by Stack Size:

Your Stack Strategy Exploit
Big Stack (Top 20%) Maximum aggression Attack medium stacks relentlessly
Medium Stack (Middle 60%) Conservative Wait for premium hands, let short stacks bust
Short Stack (Bottom 20%) Calculated risks Pick optimal spots, can't wait for premiums

Big Stack Bubble Exploitation

As a big stack on the bubble, you have tremendous fold equity. Use it ruthlessly:

  • Open raise 60-70% of buttons
  • 3-bet medium stacks liberally
  • Apply constant pressure with position
  • Force tight players to make tough decisions
  • Build massive stack for deep run

In-the-Money Play Strategy

Early ITM (Just Past Bubble)

Immediately after the bubble bursts, ICM pressure remains but shifts. Many players tighten up having secured min-cash.

Early ITM Exploitation:
  • Resume aggression immediately‚Äîdon't celebrate min-cash

  • Build chips for final table run
  • Take calculated risks with 20-40 BB stacks

Short Stack Final Table Survival

With 10-15 BB at final table, you need to pick your spot carefully but can't wait forever:

Short Stack Final Table Checklist:
  • Wait for premium spots: TT+, AQ+, AJs+
  • Shove into single opponents, avoid multi-way
  • Watch for even shorter stacks who might bust first
  • If down to 6-8 BB, widen range: 88+, A9s+, ATo+
  • Never blind out‚Äîmake a move with 5-7 BB

Chip Leader Final Table Dominance

As chip leader, you have the power to dictate final table pace and put constant pressure on medium stacks:

  • Open raise 60-70% of hands in late position
  • 3-bet medium stacks at will
  • Avoid big confrontations with 2nd-in-chips
  • Let short stacks battle each other
  • Apply relentless pressure for pay ladder climbs

Heads-Up Strategy (Playing for the Win)

Transitioning to Heads-Up Play

Heads-up poker is aggressive, with the button (small blind) holding massive advantage. Vastly widen your ranges:

  • Medium (20-50 BB): Balanced aggression, 3-bet/4-bet poker
  • Short (10-20 BB): Push/fold with wide ranges, position critical
  • Very Short (5-10 BB): Almost any ace, pair, or two broadway cards

Tournament-Specific Concepts

Chip EV vs $EV

In tournaments, maximizing chips (Chip EV) differs from maximizing prize equity ($EV). ICM bridges this gap.

Example: Final table with 20 BB, you hold AK and face all-in from opponent with 15 BB. Calling is +Chip EV (you're favorite) but may be -$EV if other short stacks could bust first and ladder you up. This is ICM consideration.

Risk Premium Calculations

The "risk premium" is extra equity you need to call compared to cash games due to ICM:

  • Early stage: Minimal risk premium (~0-5%)
  • Bubble: High risk premium (~10-20%)
  • Final table: Very high risk premium (~15-30%)
  • Heads-up: No risk premium (winner-take-all mindset)

Common Tournament Mistakes

Mistake #1: Playing Too Tight Early

Many players wait for premiums in early levels, missing value opportunities with deep stacks.

Mistake #2: Overvaluing Min-Cash

Playing scared once ITM. Top 3 payouts are 60-70% of prize pool‚Äîplay to win, not to min-cash.

Mistake #3: Ignoring Stack Sizes

Not adjusting strategy based on effective stack sizes leads to poor decisions.

Mistake #4: Passive Bubble Play with Medium Stack

Medium stacks become invisible on bubble. You need to accumulate chips for deep run.

Mistake #5: Calling Off Too Light on Final Table

Massive ICM considerations make hero calls unprofitable even when mathematically ahead.

Advanced Tournament Tactics

Re-Entry Tournament Strategy

In re-entry events, early strategy shifts toward higher variance plays:

  • Take more marginal spots early with re-entry available
  • Build big stack or re-enter, avoid medium stack grind
  • After late registration closes, revert to normal strategy
  • Consider re-entry cost in total ROI calculations

Satellite Tournament Strategy

Satellites award tournament tickets, not graduated payouts. Strategy differs dramatically:

  • Goal: Survive to ticket, not maximize chips
  • Bubble: Extreme conservatism if near guarantee
  • Short Stack: More aggressive‚Äîticket or bust
  • Big Stack: Less aggressive‚Äîprotect ticket, let others battle

Mental Game and Session Management

Tournament Variance

Tournament poker has brutal variance. Even top players experience long dry spells.

Variance Reality Check:
  • Expect to cash only 15-20% of tournaments
  • Deep runs (top 10%) happen 1-3 times per 100 tournaments
  • Wins occur every 500-1000+ tournaments (field size dependent)
  • Judge performance over 200+ tournament sample
  • Focus on decisions, not results

When to Quit a Session

Unlike cash games, tournaments have natural endpoints. However, mental fatigue matters:

  • If playing poorly after 6+ hours, make tight decisions
  • Consider selling action during long final table grinds
  • Stay hydrated and take breaks during breaks
  • Never play impaired or tilted, even deep in tournaments
