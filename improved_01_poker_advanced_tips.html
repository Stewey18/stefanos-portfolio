The 15 Advanced Tips

Intermediate Tip #1: Master Positional Awareness

Position is the single most important concept in poker that separates professionals from amateurs. Your position relative to the button determines your entire strategy.

Position Opening Range Key Strategy
Early Position (UTG, UTG+1) Top 12-15% of hands Tight ranges, strong holdings only
Middle Position (MP, MP+1) Top 18-22% of hands Balanced ranges, some speculative hands
Late Position (CO, BTN) Top 35-45% of hands Aggressive, steal blinds liberally
Blinds (SB, BB) Defensive ranges Protect investment, but fold often
Pro Tip: In late position against weak opponents, expand your opening range to include hands like K9s, Q9s, J8s, and even some suited connectors. These hands play well post-flop against players who overvalue top pair.

Advanced Tip #2: Implement GTO-Balanced Bet Sizing

Your bet sizing telegraphs information to observant opponents. Advanced players use consistent, mathematically sound bet sizes that make them difficult to exploit.

Optimal Bet Sizing Framework:

  • C-Bet on Flop: 50-75% pot (not pot-sized bets)
  • Turn Barrel: 60-75% pot
  • River Value Bet: 60-80% pot (size based on opponent calling range)
  • River Bluff: 50-60% pot (needs to work ~40% of the time)
  • 3-Bet Preflop: 3x their raise (2.5x in position)
  • 4-Bet Preflop: 2.2-2.5x their 3-bet size

Intermediate Tip #3: Attack 3-Bet Pots Aggressively

In 3-bet pots, the pot-to-stack ratio changes dramatically, requiring adjusted strategies. Most recreational players play too passively in these inflated pots.

3-Bet Pot Strategy:

Intermediate Tip #4: Respect the Power of the 4-Bet

When facing a 4-bet, you're in a high-variance situation where mistakes are costly. Understanding 4-bet ranges and proper responses is crucial.

4-Bet Defense Strategy:

  • Premium Pairs (QQ+, AK): Always continue, usually 5-bet jam or call
  • Medium Pairs (99-JJ): Stack-depth dependent, prefer folding at 100BB+
  • Suited Broadway: Consider folding unless deep-stacked (150BB+)
  • Against Tight Players: Only continue with JJ+, AK
  • Against LAG Players: Can call with 99+, AQ+, suited connectors
Key Math: A 4-bet needs to work approximately 55-60% of the time to be profitable as a bluff. If your opponent's 3-bet range is tight (10-12% of hands), 4-betting light is rarely profitable.

Intermediate Tip #6: Never Call 3-Bets Out of Position with Weak Holdings

Calling 3-bets out of position with marginal hands is one of the most common leaks among intermediate players. The math simply doesn't work.

Hands to FOLD to 3-Bets OOP:

  • Weak suited aces (A9s-A2s)
  • Offsuit broadway (KQo, KJo, QJo)
  • Small pocket pairs (22-77 at 100BB)
  • Suited connectors below 87s
  • Any hand you're "hoping to hit a flop" with

Beginner Tip #8: Stop Bluffing Recreational Players

This is perhaps the simplest yet most violated principle in online poker: recreational players don't fold enough to make bluffing profitable.

The Math: If a recreational player calls 70% of the time on the river, your bluff needs 70% fold equity to break even. Since it only gets 30% folds, you lose money every time you bluff. Against these opponents, simply bet all your value hands and check all your air‚Äîdon't bluff.

Recreational Player Identifiers:

  • VPIP (Voluntarily Put $ In Pot) above 35%
  • Won $ at Showdown above 52%
  • Limps frequently preflop
  • Plays many hours but small stakes
  • Calls rather than raises when strong

Intermediate Tip #10: Never Miss Value Bets Against Fish

Against recreational players, value betting is your primary profit source. Missing value bets costs more in EV than bad bluffs.

Value Betting Framework vs Recreational Players:

  • Top Pair or Better: Bet 3 streets for value 80% of the time
  • Middle Pair: Bet flop and turn, evaluate river
  • Weak Pair/Draw: Bet flop, check behind turn usually
  • River Sizing: Bet 60-75% pot (they call too light)
  • Thin Value: If you think you're ahead 55%+ of the time, bet

Advanced Tip #11: Respect Turn and River Aggression

When recreational players suddenly bet or raise the turn or river, they almost always have a strong hand. This is one of the most reliable tells in online poker.

Statistical Reality: When a passive recreational player (VPIP 40+, PFR 8-12) raises your turn bet, they have two pair or better approximately 85% of the time. Fold everything except strong hands yourself.

Beginner Tip #13: Use HUD Stats Effectively

A poker HUD (Heads-Up Display) shows statistical data about opponents, but you need to understand what each stat means and how to use it.

Essential HUD Stats and What They Mean:

  • VPIP (20-25%): Tight-aggressive player, respect their aggression
  • VPIP (35%+): Recreational player, value bet relentlessly
  • PFR close to VPIP: Aggressive player, 3-bet more
  • PFR half of VPIP: Passive player, isolation plays work
  • 3-Bet% (5-8%): Standard, only value hands
  • 3-Bet% (10%+): Aggressive, can 4-bet light
  • Fold to 3-Bet (60%+): Exploitable, 3-bet wide

Advanced Tip #14: Balance Your Ranges Strategically

Against observant regulars, you need balanced ranges that make you unexploitable. This means having both value hands and bluffs in similar spots.

Range Balance Example (C-Betting A-High Flops):

  • Value Hands (70%): AA, AK, AQ, AJ, AT, overpairs
  • Bluffs (30%): KQ, KJ, QJ, suited connectors, backdoor flush draws
  • Ratio: Approximately 2:1 value to bluffs
  • When to Barrel: When turn improves your bluffs (adds draws) or blank
  • When to Give Up: When turn heavily favors calling range

Advanced Concepts for Serious Players

Minimum Defense Frequency (MDF):

When facing a bet, you need to defend a minimum percentage of your range to prevent opponent from profitably bluffing any two cards.

Formula: MDF = Pot Size / (Pot Size + Bet Size)

Example: If pot is $10 and opponent bets $7, you need to continue with 10/(10+7) = 58.8% of your range.

Implied Odds Calculations:

When drawing, calculate not just pot odds but potential future winnings.

Example: You have flush draw on turn ($100 pot, opponent bets $50)

  • Direct pot odds: 3:1 (need 25% equity, you have 18%)
  • If you'll win additional $100 on river when you hit: Now 5:1 odds (need 16.7%)
  • With implied odds, the call becomes profitable
