RTP Testing & Certification

Technical Requirements Guide

Return to Player (RTP) testing ensures online casino games pay out fairly over time. All regulated games must undergo rigorous testing and certification by approved labs.

What is RTP?

RTP is the theoretical percentage of wagered money a game returns to players over time:

  • 96% RTP = $96 returned for every $100 wagered (long-term average)
  • Measured over: Millions of game rounds
  • Not guaranteed: Short-term variance is expected

Certification Process

  1. Submit game package - Complete source code, PAR sheets, math models
  2. Initial review - Lab verifies RNG, game logic, payout calculations
  3. Statistical testing - 10-100 million simulated spins
  4. Variance analysis - Verify hit frequency, volatility match specifications
  5. Security audit - Check for exploitable bugs, unauthorized features
  6. Certification issued - Game approved for specific jurisdictions

Approved Testing Labs

  • Gaming Laboratories International (GLI): Most widely accepted globally
  • BMM Testlabs: Extensive North America presence
  • eCOGRA: European focus, player protection emphasis
  • iTech Labs: Fast turnaround, competitive pricing
  • NMi Gaming: Netherlands-based, EU certifications

Ongoing Compliance

Once certified, games must maintain compliance:

  • Monthly RTP reporting to regulators
  • Annual re-certification in some jurisdictions
  • Any game changes require re-testing
  • Random audits by gaming boards

Conclusion

RTP certification protects players by ensuring games operate fairly. Testing labs conduct millions of simulations to verify payouts match specifications, with ongoing monitoring ensuring continued compliance.
