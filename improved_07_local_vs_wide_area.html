Understanding Progressive Network Types

Not all progressive jackpots are created equal. The network structure determines jackpot size, hit frequency, base RTP, and ultimately your expected value. Let's break down each type.

Standalone Progressives

How They Work

Single machine with independent jackpot meter. Only bets on that specific machine feed the progressive pool.

Characteristic Standalone Details
Typical Jackpot Range $1,000 - $50,000
Growth Rate Slow (only your casino's play)
Hit Frequency High (1 in 100K - 1M spins)
Base RTP 92-95% (better than network)
Competition Low (single machine)
Contribution % 5-10% of each bet
Standalone Advantage: You're not competing against thousands of players worldwide. If you're the only person playing that machine, every spin builds YOUR jackpot exclusively.

Local Area Progressives (LAP)

How They Work

Multiple machines within single casino or gaming site linked together. All linked machines contribute to and compete for the same jackpot.

Characteristic Local Area Details
Typical Jackpot Range $10,000 - $500,000
Growth Rate Medium (casino-wide traffic)
Hit Frequency Medium (1 in 1M - 10M spins)
Base RTP 89-93%
Competition Medium (10-100 machines)
Contribution % 2-5% of each bet

LAP Examples

  • Casino floor progressives linking 20-50 machines
  • Online casino proprietary progressives (e.g., Betsson's own network)
  • Regional progressives (all casinos in one state)

Wide Area Progressives (WAP)

How They Work

Massive networks linking hundreds or thousands of machines across multiple casinos, often globally. Every player on any linked machine worldwide contributes to and competes for the same mega jackpot.

Characteristic Wide Area Details
Typical Jackpot Range $1M - $40M+
Growth Rate Fast (global player base)
Hit Frequency Very Low (1 in 50M - 100M spins)
Base RTP 85-89% (lowest of all types)
Competition Extreme (thousands of players)
Contribution % 1-3% of each bet

Famous WAP Networks

  • Microgaming Progressive Network: Mega Moolah, Major Millions, King Cashalot
  • NetEnt Network: Mega Fortune, Arabian Nights, Hall of Gods
  • Playtech Network: Age of the Gods, Jackpot Giant
  • IGT MegaJackpots: Cleopatra, Wheel of Fortune, Siberian Storm

Expected Value Calculations

Standalone Progressive Math

Example: $20,000 Standalone
Hit frequency: 1 in 500,000 spins
Jackpot contribution: $20,000 √∑ 500,000 = $0.04 per spin
Base RTP: 94%
Total RTP: 94% + 4% = 98%

Verdict: Reasonable value, playable long-term

Local Area Progressive Math

Example: $100,000 LAP
Hit frequency: 1 in 5,000,000 spins
Jackpot contribution: $100,000 √∑ 5,000,000 = $0.02 per spin
Base RTP: 91%
Total RTP: 91% + 2% = 93%

Verdict: Poor value, high house edge

Wide Area Progressive Math

Example: $5M Wide Area
Hit frequency: 1 in 50,000,000 spins
Jackpot contribution: $5,000,000 √∑ 50,000,000 = $0.10 per spin
Base RTP: 88%
Total RTP: 88% + 10% = 98%

Verdict: Good theoretical value, but requires infinite bankroll/time

Which Type Should You Play?

Play Standalone If:

  • You have limited bankroll ($100-500)
  • You value consistent smaller wins over lottery shots
  • You want better base RTP and lower variance
  • You prefer realistic win potential
  • You're playing to maximize entertainment time

Play Local Area If:

  • You want middle-ground jackpots ($50K-200K)
  • You're at a busy casino with active progressive networks
  • You can track meter history to identify ripe jackpots
  • You're seeking six-figure potential without lottery odds
Reality Check: Local area progressives often provide the WORST value. They have worse base RTP than standalone but don't offer the massive upside of wide-area networks. Only play LAP when jackpot is significantly above average.

Play Wide Area If:

  • You're playing for life-changing money only
  • You have substantial bankroll ($1,000+)
  • You understand you'll almost certainly lose
  • You want lottery-style excitement
  • The jackpot is 2x+ average size

Network Progressive Myths

Myth #1: "Wide area progressives hit more often"

Reality: They hit more frequently in absolute terms (more players = more spins), but YOUR individual odds are lowest with wide-area networks.

Myth #2: "Local progressives are better value"

Reality: LAP often provides worst value‚Äîlower RTP than standalone without the massive upside of WAP.

Myth #3: "Jackpot size indicates better value"

Reality: A $20K standalone at 1 in 500K odds gives better expected value than $5M at 1 in 50M odds.

Analyzing Your Local Casino's Progressives

Questions to Ask

  1. What's the network type? (Standalone, LAP, or WAP)
  2. How many machines are linked? (More machines = lower individual odds)
  3. What's the current jackpot vs. average? (Only play if 20%+ above average)
  4. When did it last hit? (Longer drought might indicate overdue)
  5. What's the base RTP? (Should be posted, usually 85-95%)
  6. What's the minimum bet requirement? (Lower minimum = better value)

Conclusion

The progressive type matters more than the game itself. A standalone progressive offers significantly better mathematical value than a wide-area network game, even if the WAP has a $10M jackpot.

For most players, standalone progressives provide the best combination of entertainment value, reasonable RTP, and achievable wins. Local area progressives often represent the worst value‚Äîavoid unless the jackpot is exceptionally high.

Wide-area progressives like Mega Moolah should be treated as lottery tickets: play for fun with money you can afford to lose, bet minimum stakes, and recognize you're buying excitement rather than value.
