Gaming Control Board Auditing Process

Complete Technical Guide to Regulatory Audits

Gaming Control Boards conduct comprehensive audits of online casino operators to ensure compliance with regulations, protect players, and maintain industry integrity. This guide explains the complete auditing process, what auditors review, and how operators should prepare.

Types of Regulatory Audits

1. Initial Licensing Audit

Conducted before license approval:

  • Scope: Complete business review, background checks, financial analysis, technical testing
  • Duration: 3-6 months
  • Cost: Operator pays $50K-$300K in audit fees
  • Outcome: License approved, conditionally approved, or denied

2. Annual Compliance Audit

Comprehensive yearly review:

  • Scope: Financial audit, game testing, responsible gaming compliance, AML review
  • Duration: 2-4 weeks on-site + report writing
  • Frequency: Required annually for license renewal
  • Notice: Usually 30-60 days advance notice

3. Random Spot Audits

Unannounced compliance checks:

  • Scope: Focused review of specific areas (RNG output, payout percentages, player funds)
  • Duration: 1-3 days
  • Frequency: 1-3 times per year, no advance notice
  • Trigger: Random selection or complaint-driven

4. Triggered Investigations

Investigations prompted by specific concerns:

  • Triggers: Player complaints, unusual financial patterns, system failures, security breaches
  • Scope: Focused on specific issue
  • Duration: Days to months depending on severity
  • Outcome: Can result in fines, license suspension, or revocation

The Annual Audit Process

Phase 1: Pre-Audit Preparation (30-60 days before)

Regulator Actions:

  • Sends official audit notice with scope and document requests
  • Assigns audit team (typically 3-5 auditors)
  • Reviews previous audit findings and operator responses

Operator Requirements:

  • Prepare requested documentation (financial records, logs, reports)
  • Schedule key personnel availability
  • Conduct internal pre-audit to identify issues
  • Prepare workspace for audit team

Phase 2: On-Site Audit (1-3 weeks)

Week 1: Financial Review

  • Examination of financial statements and tax filings
  • Revenue reconciliation (gross gaming revenue calculation)
  • Player fund segregation verification
  • Payout percentage verification
  • Bonus liability accuracy

Week 2: Technical Review

  • RNG testing (request random output samples)
  • Game integrity checks (compare certified versions to live)
  • Server access controls review
  • Disaster recovery testing
  • Geolocation accuracy verification

Week 3: Compliance Review

  • Responsible gaming tool functionality testing
  • Age verification process review
  • AML transaction monitoring review
  • Self-exclusion list cross-checking
  • Advertising compliance review

Phase 3: Exit Interview & Initial Findings (Day 15-20)

  • Audit team presents preliminary findings to operator management
  • Discussion of identified issues and potential violations
  • Operator provides immediate responses or explanations
  • Agreement on timeline for formal report

Phase 4: Audit Report & Operator Response (30-60 days post-audit)

  • Regulator issues formal audit report with findings
  • Findings categorized: Critical, Major, Minor
  • Operator has 30 days to respond with corrective action plan
  • Follow-up audit may be scheduled for critical findings

Compliance Areas

  • Responsible Gaming: Test deposit limits, time limits, self-exclusion, reality checks
  • AML: Review suspicious transaction reports, large transaction handling, customer due diligence
  • Advertising: Review all marketing materials for compliance with state rules
  • Customer Service: Review complaint handling, response times, resolution rates

Common Audit Findings

Critical Findings (License at Risk)

  • Commingling player funds with operating funds
  • RNG manipulation or unapproved game modifications
  • Allowing play from restricted jurisdictions
  • Failure to honor withdrawals
  • Inadequate AML controls (egregious cases)

Major Findings (Fines Likely)

  • Incorrect GGR reporting (under-reporting taxes)
  • Responsible gaming tools not functioning properly
  • Age verification failures
  • Self-exclusion list not properly implemented
  • Failure to submit required reports on time

Minor Findings (Corrective Action Required)

  • Incomplete record keeping
  • Minor advertising compliance issues
  • Documentation errors
  • Policy updates needed

Operator Preparation Checklist

30 Days Before Audit

  • ☐ Review all requested documents for completeness
  • ☐ Run internal RNG tests to identify any issues
  • ☐ Verify all games match certified versions
  • ☐ Test all responsible gaming tools
  • ☐ Review AML transaction logs for anomalies
  • ☐ Prepare reconciliation of player funds
  • ☐ Update all compliance policies to current versions

Week Before Audit

  • ☐ Organize all documentation in logical structure
  • ☐ Brief key personnel on their roles
  • ☐ Set up dedicated workspace for auditors
  • ☐ Ensure system access for auditors (read-only)
  • ☐ Prepare list of recent system changes or issues

During Audit

  • ☐ Assign liaison to coordinate with audit team
  • ☐ Respond to document requests within 24 hours
  • ☐ Keep detailed notes of all discussions
  • ☐ Ask for clarification on unclear findings
  • ☐ Begin drafting responses to preliminary findings

Best Practices for Audit Success

  1. Proactive Compliance: Conduct quarterly internal audits to identify issues before regulators find them
  2. Documentation: Maintain meticulous records of all decisions, changes, and incidents
  3. Transparency: Disclose issues voluntarily rather than waiting for auditors to discover them
  4. Training: Ensure all staff understand compliance requirements
  5. Technology: Invest in automated compliance monitoring tools
  6. Relationship: Maintain open communication with regulators year-round

Conclusion

Gaming Control Board audits are comprehensive examinations designed to protect players and ensure industry integrity. Operators who maintain strong internal compliance programs, prepare thoroughly, and cooperate fully with auditors typically complete audits successfully with minimal findings.

Key success factors: Accurate financial reporting, tested technical systems, functioning compliance tools, complete documentation, and transparent communication with regulators.
