Blackjack vs Poker: Which Should You Play?

The short answer: If you want better odds and faster games, play blackjack. If you want higher earning potential and don't mind losing for months while you learn, play poker.

But like everything in gambling, it's not that simple. Let's break down both games honestly so you can decide which fits your goals, bankroll, and personality.

The Quick Comparison

Factor Blackjack Poker
House Edge 0.5% (with strategy) 0% (you play other players)
Learning Curve 1-2 weeks 6 months to years
Can You Beat It? No (except card counting) Yes (skill beats luck long-term)
Minimum Bankroll $100-500 $1,000-5,000
Speed of Play Fast (60-80 hands/hour) Slow (20-30 hands/hour)

Already see which fits you better? Let's dig deeper.

Blackjack: The Mathematician's Game

How It Works

You play against the dealer, not other players. Goal: get closer to 21 than the dealer without going over. With perfect basic strategy, the house edge drops to 0.5% - the best odds in any casino game.

PROS:

  • Easy to learn - memorize basic strategy chart in a weekend
  • Best odds in the casino (0.5% house edge)
  • Fast-paced - play 60-80 hands per hour
  • Low variance compared to other games
  • No emotional decisions - just follow the chart
  • Works great with small bankroll ($100+)

CONS:

  • You can't beat it long-term (unless counting cards)
  • House always has the edge
  • Gets boring after a while - mechanical play
  • No real strategy decisions beyond basic
  • Card counting is hard and gets you banned
  • Limited earning potential

Who Should Play Blackjack?

You're a good fit if:

  • You want entertainment, not a career
  • You like clear rules with no grey area
  • You have limited bankroll ($100-1,000)
  • You want the best odds possible
  • You prefer solo play over social games
  • You don't want to study for months

Expected Results

With perfect basic strategy, expect to lose about $0.50 per $100 wagered. That's it. Play $25 per hand for 100 hands ($2,500 total wagered) and you'll lose about $12.50 on average.

Reality check: Short-term variance means you can still win. I've had sessions where I turned $200 into $800. But play long enough and the math catches up. You will lose.

Poker: The Skill Game

How It Works

You play against other players, not the house. The casino just takes a small rake (usually 5-10% of each pot). With enough skill, you can consistently beat weaker players and make money long-term.

PROS:

  • You CAN beat it and make consistent profit
  • No house edge - just rake
  • Unlimited earning potential
  • Highly strategic with endless depth
  • Social and competitive
  • Rewarding to master

CONS:

  • Takes 6-12 months to get decent
  • Takes years to get good
  • Requires serious bankroll ($1,000+ minimum)
  • High variance - expect to lose for weeks/months
  • Emotionally draining
  • Slow-paced compared to blackjack
  • Dealing with tilting, bad beats, and frustration

Who Should Play Poker?

You're a good fit if:

  • You're willing to study seriously
  • You have proper bankroll ($1,000-5,000 minimum)
  • You can handle losing streaks emotionally
  • You're competitive and want to improve
  • You have patience for slow games
  • You want to actually beat the game

Expected Results

Beginner poker players lose. Period. Expect to lose $500-2,000 in your first 6 months while learning. After that, if you're good, you can make $10-30/hour at low stakes ($1/2 games).

Advanced players at mid-stakes ($2/5-$5/10) can make $50-150/hour. High-stakes pros make hundreds of thousands or millions per year.

But here's reality: Most players never become profitable. About 10% of poker players win long-term. The other 90% are the fish feeding the sharks.

Head-to-Head: Key Factors

1. Earning Potential

WINNER: POKER

Blackjack has a 0.5% house edge. You WILL lose over time. Poker lets skilled players win consistently. If you're serious about making money gambling, poker is the only realistic option.

2. Learning Difficulty

WINNER: BLACKJACK

Blackjack basic strategy takes a weekend to learn. Poker takes years to master. If you want quick entertainment, blackjack is way easier.

3. Bankroll Requirements

WINNER: BLACKJACK

You can play blackjack with $100. Poker needs $1,000-5,000 minimum to handle variance. Blackjack is way more accessible.

4. Immediate Fun

WINNER: BLACKJACK

Blackjack is fast, simple, and fun right away. Poker is slow, frustrating when you're learning, and involves a lot of folding.

5. Long-term Profitability

WINNER: POKER

Only poker offers realistic path to consistent profits. Blackjack is pure entertainment - you can't beat it long-term without card counting.

6. Stress Level

WINNER: BLACKJACK

Blackjack is low-stress. Follow the chart, accept the variance. Poker involves constant decision-making, dealing with bad beats, and managing tilt.

The Hybrid Approach

Here's what I actually recommend: Play both.

Use blackjack for casual entertainment and comped drinks. Use poker when you're serious and want to put in the work to potentially make money.

My personal split: 80% poker, 20% blackjack. Poker is my main game where I try to win. Blackjack is what I play when I just want to relax and don't want to think.

THE FINAL VERDICT

Play Blackjack if: You want entertainment with the best odds. You have limited bankroll. You want something simple and fast.

Play Poker if: You're willing to study and improve. You have proper bankroll. You want earning potential and don't mind the stress.

Play Both if: You're smart. Different games for different moods. Blackjack for casual fun, poker for serious play.

Common Questions

Q: Can I count cards in blackjack and beat it?
Technically yes, but it's extremely hard. You need perfect execution, big bankroll, and you'll get banned quickly. Unless you're Rain Man, forget it.

Q: How long until I'm profitable at poker?
Most people never get there. If you study hard and play regularly, expect 6-12 months minimum. And that's just to break even.

Q: Which game has less variance?
Blackjack. Way less variance. Poker has wild swings - you can play perfectly and still lose for weeks.

Q: Can I play both online?
Yes. Online blackjack and poker are both legal in NJ, PA, MI, WV, and a few other states.

Q: Which makes more money?
Poker has way higher earning potential IF you get good. But most people make $0 at poker because they never improve.

Bottom Line

Blackjack is for entertainment. Poker is for competition and potential profit.

If you just want to gamble casually and have fun, play blackjack. Best odds, simple rules, low stress.

If you want to actually make money gambling and you're willing to put in months of study, play poker. It's the only casino game you can actually beat.

But honestly? Most people should just play blackjack. It's fun, easy, and you won't lose your shirt as quickly. Save poker for when you're ready to get serious.
