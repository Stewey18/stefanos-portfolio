How Roulette Works

The game is beautifully simple: a croupier spins the roulette wheel in one direction and releases a small ball in the opposite direction. As the wheel slows, the ball eventually settles into one of the numbered pockets. If you've placed a bet on that number, color, or range of numbers, you win!

The Roulette Wheel Numbers

Red Numbers: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 12, 14, 16, 18, 19, 21, 23, 25, 27, 30, 32, 34, 36
Black Numbers: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 11, 13, 15, 17, 20, 22, 24, 26, 28, 29, 31, 33, 35
Green (Zero): 0 (and 00 in American Roulette)

Types of Bets

Roulette offers a wide variety of betting options, divided into two main categories:

Inside Bets (Higher Risk, Higher Reward)

Straight Up

Bet on a single number. The riskiest but most rewarding bet.

Coverage: 1 number

Pays 35:1

Split Bet

Bet on two adjacent numbers by placing chips on the line between them.

Coverage: 2 numbers

Pays 17:1

Street Bet

Bet on three numbers in a horizontal row.

Coverage: 3 numbers

Pays 11:1

Corner Bet

Bet on four numbers that meet at one corner by placing chips at the intersection.

Coverage: 4 numbers

Pays 8:1

Line Bet

Bet on six numbers from two adjacent rows.

Coverage: 6 numbers

Pays 5:1

Outside Bets (Lower Risk, More Frequent Wins)

Red or Black

Bet on all red or all black numbers. One of the most popular bets.

Coverage: 18 numbers

Pays 1:1

Odd or Even

Bet on all odd or all even numbers.

Coverage: 18 numbers

Pays 1:1

High or Low

Bet on low numbers (1-18) or high numbers (19-36).

Coverage: 18 numbers

Pays 1:1

Dozens

Bet on first dozen (1-12), second dozen (13-24), or third dozen (25-36).

Coverage: 12 numbers

Pays 2:1

Columns

Bet on one of three vertical columns of 12 numbers each.

Coverage: 12 numbers

Pays 2:1
